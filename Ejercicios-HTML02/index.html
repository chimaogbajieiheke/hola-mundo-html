World Wide Web

The World Wide Web (WWW) or Global Computer Network, is an information system where
documents and other web resources are identified by URLs,
interlinked by hypertext links, and accessible via the Internet.
It was invented by Tim Berners-Lee in 1989 while working at CERN.
The web has been one of the most important technologies in human history,
revolutionizing communication, commerce, and entertainment.
It has transformed the way we access and share information,
and has had a profound impact on society and culture.
The web continues to evolve, with new technologies and applications being developed all the time.
It remains a powerful tool for connecting people and ideas around the world.
